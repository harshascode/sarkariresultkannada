### Bad Newz Review – A Rom-Com with a Promising Start but a Rocky Finish
Bad Newz, the latest romantic comedy starring Vicky Kaushal and Triptii Dimri, offers a mixed bag of entertainment and drama. The film starts off on a high note, with its first half filled with humor and charm, but unfortunately, it loses its way in the second half, succumbing to melodrama. Set against the backdrop of modern relationships and personal ambitions, the movie explores interesting themes but often gets tangled in its own narrative.

The story follows Akhil Chadha, played by Vicky Kaushal, a character whose over-the-top personality and complicated love life form the crux of the film's comedic elements. His love interest, Saloni, played by Triptii Dimri, is a talented chef with aspirations of culinary fame. The first half of the movie is where it truly shines, with light-hearted and silly moments that keep the audience engaged. The chemistry between Kaushal and Dimri is undeniable, and their performances are delightful, bringing a certain energy to the screen that is hard to look away from.

However, as the film progresses, it takes a dramatic turn, shifting from comedy to melodrama. The second half is filled with emotional moments that, while intended to add depth, feel forced and jarring, disrupting the fun tone established earlier. The exploration of themes such as relationships, parenthood, and ambition is commendable, but the execution often falls short, making the pacing feel uneven.

### Technical Aspects
The technical aspects of the film, including cinematography and music, play a crucial role in setting the mood and pacing. The cinematography is vibrant and captures the essence of the characters' worlds, especially in the culinary scenes where Saloni's passion for cooking comes alive. The music, while not overly memorable, complements the comedic and emotional moments, though it sometimes feels like it's trying too hard to elevate the mood. The pacing and editing are where the film falters, as the transition from comedy to drama feels abrupt and affects the overall flow of the movie.

### Performances & Characters
Vicky Kaushal and Triptii Dimri are the standout performers of the film, bringing their characters to life with their energetic and engaging performances. Kaushal's comedic timing is impeccable, and Dimri's portrayal of a driven and loving partner is relatable and endearing. The supporting cast, including Ammy Virk, Sheeba Chadha, and Neha Dhupia, add to the film's humor and emotional depth, though some characters feel underutilized.

### My Rating & Final Verdict
I would give Bad Newz a rating of 3/5. While it has its flaws, particularly in its narrative shift and pacing issues, the film also has memorable moments, thanks to the performances of its lead cast. For viewers looking for light entertainment with a mix of comedy and drama, Bad Newz might be worth a watch, especially for fans of Vicky Kaushal and Triptii Dimri. However, those seeking a consistent tone and depth in their romantic comedies might find themselves slightly disappointed. Nonetheless, the film's attempt to tackle serious themes amidst its comedic setup is commendable, making it a decent addition to the romantic comedy genre.
