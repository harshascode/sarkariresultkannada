### Prasanna Vadanam Review – A Gripping Thriller with a Unique Twist

#### Movie Summary & Story

Prasanna Vadanam, a Telugu suspense-thriller film, delves into the captivating story of Surya, a radio jockey suffering from Prosopagnosia, also known as Face Blindness. This condition makes it impossible for him to recognize faces, which becomes a significant challenge when he witnesses a murder. With the help of ACP Vaidehi, Surya navigates a suspenseful journey to uncover the truth behind the murder. The film combines elements of drama and thriller, keeping the audience engaged with its intricate plot and unexpected twists. The stakes are high, and the tension builds up as Surya tries to identify the killer despite his condition. The story is set against the backdrop of everyday life, making it relatable and realistic. The rural setting adds to the authenticity of the narrative, providing a unique perspective on the protagonist's struggles.

#### Technical Aspects

The film's technical aspects are noteworthy, with Vijay Bulganin's music enhancing the critical moments and creating a tense atmosphere. The background score is well-composed, adding depth to the narrative. S Chandrasekaran's cinematography is commendable, capturing the essence of the story and providing a visual treat. The pacing and editing, handled by Karthika Srinivas, are generally well-done, although some scenes feel a bit stretched. The film could benefit from a tighter, faster-paced screenplay to keep the audience engaged throughout.

#### Performances & Characters

The lead cast delivers impressive performances, with Suhas Pagolu shining as Surya, the protagonist struggling with Face Blindness. He showcases his versatility in this emotionally intense role, bringing depth to the character. Rashi Singh takes a bold step in portraying a negative character, ACP Vaidehi, and her performance adds depth to the narrative. Payal Radhakrishna and Viva Mr Amona give solid performances in their respective roles, while Nitin Prasanna leaves a mark in his supporting role. The chemistry between the lead actors is palpable, making their interactions believable and engaging.

#### My Rating & Final Verdict

I would give Prasanna Vadanam a rating of 3 out of 5 stars. The film is a gripping thriller with a unique twist, and the lead cast delivers impressive performances. Although it has some pacing issues and unnecessary scenes, the film's technical aspects and suspenseful moments make it a worthwhile watch. If you're a fan of suspense-thrillers, Prasanna Vadanam is definitely worth checking out. However, if you're looking for a flawless narrative with no pacing issues, you might want to consider other options. Overall, the film is a decent addition to the Telugu suspense-thriller genre, and its unique plot and impressive performances make it a must-watch for fans of the genre.
