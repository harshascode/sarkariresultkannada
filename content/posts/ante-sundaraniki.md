## Ante Sundaraniki! Review – When Little Lies Lead to Big Laughs  



### Movie Summary & Story  

*Ante Sundaraniki!* follows the unlikely romance between Sundar (Nani), a straight‑laced Brahmin from Hyderabad, and Leela (Nazriya Nazim), a free‑spirited Christian girl living in the United States. Their love story begins at a cultural exchange program, where the two discover that their families’ expectations are as rigid as the religious boundaries that separate them.  

Instead of confronting the clash head‑on, Sundar and Leela embark on a series of increasingly elaborate deceptions—pretending to be from the same faith, inventing fake relatives, and even staging a fake wedding—to win approval from both sides. Each lie spawns another, creating a domino effect of comedic misunderstandings and heartfelt moments. The stakes rise when the families start planning actual ceremonies, forcing the couple to confront the truth behind their masquerade.  

The film’s non‑linear narrative weaves flashbacks of childhood traditions, interfaith festivals, and family gatherings, giving the audience a clear picture of why the couple feels compelled to hide their love. Themes of tradition versus modernity, the weight of parental expectations, and the humor that can arise from cultural friction are explored with a light‑hearted yet sincere tone. By the climax, the lies that once protected the lovers become the very barriers they must dismantle, delivering an emotional payoff that feels earned rather than contrived.

### Technical Aspects  

**Cinematography** – P. G. Venkatesh captures the contrasting worlds of Hyderabad’s bustling streets and the sun‑kissed suburbs of the United States with equal affection. The camera often lingers on the intricate details of Brahmin rituals—hand‑painted rangolis, the gleam of temple lamps—while juxtaposing them with the pastel‑toned, open‑air cafés where Leela works. This visual dichotomy reinforces the film’s central conflict without resorting to heavy exposition.  

**Music & BGM** – Vivek Sagar’s soundtrack is a vibrant blend of traditional Carnatic motifs and contemporary indie pop. The opening theme, “Madhurame,” uses a veena riff that subtly morphs into an acoustic guitar, mirroring the protagonists’ cultural blend. Background scores during the “lie‑building” sequences employ playful percussive loops, heightening the comedic timing. In the emotional crescendo, Sagar switches to a minimalist piano, allowing the actors’ performances to breathe. The songs are well‑placed, never feeling like a forced commercial interlude.  

**Pacing & Editing** – Naveen Nooli’s editing maintains a brisk rhythm for the first two acts, ensuring the rapid-fire jokes land with precision. However, the third act stretches into a longer, more contemplative segment where the consequences of the deceptions unfold. While this slowdown adds emotional depth, it also contributes to the film’s 150‑minute runtime, which some viewers may find a tad indulgent. Overall, the pacing strikes a balance between laugh‑out‑loud moments and heartfelt reflection.



### Performances & Characters  

**Nani (Sundar)** – Nani delivers a nuanced performance that oscillates between earnest sincerity and mischievous schemer. His comic timing shines in scenes where he juggles multiple personas, yet his quieter moments—especially when confronting his father’s expectations—reveal a subtle vulnerability that grounds the narrative.  

**Nazriya Nazim (Leela)** – Making her Telugu debut, Nazriya brings a fresh energy to Leela. Her expressive eyes convey the internal tug‑of‑war between familial loyalty and personal desire. The chemistry with Nani feels natural, and her comedic improvisations—particularly during the “fake baptism” scene—add layers to the character’s boldness.  

**Supporting Cast** – The film benefits from a strong ensemble. Sundar’s father (played by Rao Ramesh) embodies the stern yet caring patriarch, delivering memorable lines about tradition without feeling caricatured. Leela’s mother (Madhavi Latha) provides comic relief with her over‑protective antics, while the quirky friend (Vennela Kishore) acts as the catalyst for many of the couple’s absurd plans. Each supporting role feels purposeful, reinforcing the central theme that families, despite their quirks, are ultimately rooted in love.  



### Box Office & Collection  

*Ante Sundaraniki!* opened on June 10, 2022, amid a crowded summer slate. Domestic collections were modest, with the film struggling to break the ₹20 crore mark in the first week. However, overseas territories—particularly the United Arab Emirates, United States, and Australia—responded positively, contributing significantly to the worldwide gross of over ₹45 crore. While the film did not recoup its production budget domestically, the cumulative earnings and subsequent streaming success on Netflix helped it achieve a respectable overall recovery.  



### What Works & What Doesn't  

**Pros**  
- **Sharp, relatable humor** rooted in cultural nuances.  
- **Nani and Nazriya’s chemistry**, making the romance believable.  
- **Vivek Sagar’s music**, which seamlessly blends tradition with modern beats.  
- **Cinematography** that visually distinguishes the two worlds.  
- **Heartfelt moments** that prevent the comedy from feeling shallow.  

**Cons**  
- **Lengthy runtime**, especially in the final act, can test patience.  
- **Some sub‑plots** (e.g., the side‑character’s love story) feel under‑developed.  
- **Pacing dip** during the climax, where the narrative slows for emotional exposition.  



### My Rating & Final Verdict  

**Rating:** ★★★★☆ (4 out of 5)  

*Ante Sundaraniki!* succeeds where many rom‑coms stumble: it respects the cultural backdrop while delivering universal laughs. The film’s heart lies in its honest portrayal of families trying to protect their own, even if their methods are hilariously misguided. Though the runtime could have been tighter, the combination of strong performances, witty writing, and a soundtrack that elevates every mood makes it a delightful watch.  

**Verdict:** If you enjoy a rom‑com that balances humor with genuine emotion and offers a fresh take on interfaith relationships, *Ante Sundaraniki!* is well worth your time—both on the big screen and on streaming platforms.
